The Ultimate Notebook Accounting System

Your side hustle is bringing in some money and that is AWESOME. Now that you've got some regular income it is time to start keeping track of it. Don't worry if you don't know much about "accounting". You don't need to!

Let's take a look at an incredibly simple way to keep track of your side business income that requires no more than some paper, a pen, and a little discipline (aka, the hard part).

Why Track Income?

If you are reading this article you probably already know that there are many reasons to keep track of income from your side business. If you still need some convincing, here are a few reasons of my own:

Get peace of mind.

Keeping good records will make you feel in control of your business finances and avoid related stress. You shouldn't have to wonder if you forgot to collect payment or struggle to figure out what your clients owe you.

Be professional.

It should be a simple matter to answer client questions about their outstanding balance. Similarly, it should be easy to answer your own (or your accountant's) questions about how much money is coming in. A professional business knows the answer to these questions at all times and you should too.

See your progress.

Tracking the money you have made with your side hustle will highlight its postive impact on your finances and provide motivation to keep at it.

Save money at the accountant.

Any accountant will tell you that the best way to save money on accounting is to be as organized as possible.

Required Equipment

You've probably already got what you need to get up and running:

Required: Paper and a pen.
Optional: Notebook or binder. 3-hole punch. Ruler.

Shortcut: Use a printer to print out the free template!

Getting Started

Each of your clients gets their own sheet of paper. Let’s go ahead and set up one client:

  1. Write the client name up in the top right corner of the page.
  2. The individual transactions will go into a table. Split the page into the following 4 columns:
    • Date - the transaction date
    • Description - what happened?
    • Amount - the amount of this transaction
    • Balance - the balance after this transaction

This is the most basic set up. See the “Pro Tips” section below for some ideas that expand on this.

How To Use

With your page set up, you are ready to start keeping track of your income.

When you do work:

Add a new row in your table:

  1. Write the date
  2. Enter a description of the work
  3. Enter the amount charged
  4. Update the “balance” column by adding the amount to the previous “balance” value.

When you get paid:

Add a new row in your table:

  1. Write the date
  2. Enter a description of the payment (I.e. “Cash Payment”, “Check Payment”)
  3. Enter the amount paid as a negative number
  4. Update the “balance” column by subtracting the amount paid from the previous “balance” value.

That's it!

Pro Tips

Check out the following tips to get the most out of your notebook accounting system.

Use A Binder

Keep your system flexible by using loose sheets and putting them in a 3-ring binder. That way it's easy to insert pages and reorganize things if need be. You can also use tabbed separators to easily look up your clients.

Add A Summary

When you wrap up a page, summarize the activity on that page at the top. Write the date range, the starting and ending balance and total up the payments on the page.

Get More Column Space

Running out of room in your columns? Turning the notebook sideways will give you more space to write descriptions and make room for any "custom" columns.

Track Hourly Work

You can easily track hourly work using a similar approach with some minor modifications.

When you set up your page, add two columns to the basic setup:

  1. Rate - Price charged per hour
  2. Hours - Hours worked
Simply enter the rate and hours and multiply them together to get the amount. For payments, just leave the rate and hours columns empty.

Total Payments

Want to know how much money you have been paid by a client at all times? Add a "total payments" column to your table and keep a running total by adding the amount of payment transactions.

Scan Pages

Use Evernote to digitize your income notebook. "Scan" your pages into Evernote with your mobile phone and it will not only give you a digital backup of your paper system but it also uses OCR to make your handwriting searchable. How cool is that?!

Fix Wide Margins In Template

Using the free downloadable template? Print in "actual size" to avoid extra wide margins.
